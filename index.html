About me

I am a doctoral student and member of the Data Science laboratory at the University of Ljubljana, Faculty of Computer and Information Science. I am supervised by prof. dr. Erik Štrumbelj. I am also a teaching assistant in the areas of probability and statistics, as well as a fellow of the American Slovenian Education Foundation.
My doctoral research deals with normalizing flows in Markov Chain Monte Carlo for sampling from challenging probability distributions. My work intersects with research in cosmological and astronomical inference. My research interests include machine learning, generative modeling, normalizing flows, Bayesian statistics, and Markov Chain Monte Carlo. I also have expertise and experience in various domains such as: data and uncertainty visualization, predictive maintenance, social media analytics, sports analytics, and medical analytics.
Check out my most recent projects on my Github page.
